What happens when you initialize a repository? Why do you need to do it?

You create an empty repository. You need it to store all the future commits of your project and track changes.

How is the staging area different from the working directory and the repository? What value do you think it offers?

It is a temporary working space like a scratch board, while working directory and repository are permanant working space. You could use it to test new changes and easily revert to a previous commit.

How can you use the staging area to make sure you have one commit per logical change?

after a logical change is made, you can commit the change.

What are some situations when branches would be helpful in keeping your history organized? How would branches help?

When you want to test new ideas and making other versions. Branches could help to track changes going into a certian direction.

How do the diagrams help you visualize the branch structure?

Showing the workflow and reachability.

What is the result of merging two branches together? Why do we represent it in the diagram the way we do?

implement changes introduced by both branches on their common ancester.

What are the pros and cons of Git’s automatic merging vs. always doing merges manually?

depending on the scenarios, sometime the automatic merging is very convinient, but it could cause trouble in other more complicated scenarios.
